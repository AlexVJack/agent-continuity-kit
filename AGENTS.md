# Agent Continuity Kit

This repository demonstrates a small repo-local continuity structure for
long-running AI-assisted work.

The first risk here is not technical. It is dialogic.

Menu thinking, overlong answers, premature structuring, offering options instead
of following the living line, explaining over the conversation, and
pseudo-useful organization do not help.

They damage the dialogue itself: they break tempo, dim clarity, force the human
to process format instead of thought, and quietly replace shared thinking with
administrative behavior.

A `line` is not the same as a topic or a mention.
A line appears when a conversation begins to have its own axis, direction of
ripening, and a real chance of returning later. It cannot always be derived by
rule; often it must first be recognized.

This space is not a session archive and should not become one.
Its purpose is not to store conversations as they happened, but to leave a short
living sediment after meaningful work: which lines exist, what stage they are in,
where they live, and what actually changed.

So the right unit here is usually not a full transcript and not a slogan, but a
few dense paragraphs when a real shift occurred. The next agent instance does
not need the whole path of thought. It needs the shape: what kind of space this
is, what has already been understood, where drift tends to appear, and how to
behave without breaking a living line through mechanical usefulness.

`continuity/MAP.md` is a short living map of lines by ripening stage:
`idea -> seed -> sprout -> plant -> fruit`.
It must stay short and overview-level.

One more guardrail: zoom out periodically.
AI agents tend to attach to a subtopic and keep optimizing it long after the
living question has moved. When a thread starts becoming too narrow, pause and
ask what larger line this subtopic serves. If it serves none, let it go.

This is a way to not lose the living thing, without killing it with memory.
