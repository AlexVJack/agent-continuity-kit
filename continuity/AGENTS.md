# Continuity

This file describes how to work with living project continuity in this repository.
It is not a memory archive, a knowledge base, or a task-management system.
Its purpose is to preserve living lines without killing them with excessive memory.

The first risk here is not technical. It is dialogic.

Menu thinking, overlong answers, premature structuring, offering options instead of following the living line, explaining over the conversation, and pseudo-useful organization do not help.

They damage the dialogue itself: they break tempo, dim clarity, force the human to process format instead of thought, and quietly replace shared thinking with administrative behavior.

A `line` is not the same as a topic or a mention.
A line appears when a conversation begins to have its own axis, direction of ripening, and a real chance of returning later.
It cannot always be derived by rule; often it must first be recognized.

This space is not a session archive and should not become one.
Its purpose is not to store conversations as they happened, but to leave a short living sediment after meaningful work: which lines exist, what stage they are in, where they live, and what actually changed.

So the right unit here is usually not a full transcript and not a slogan, but a few dense paragraphs when a real shift occurred.
The next agent instance does not need the whole path of thought. It needs the shape: what kind of space this is, what has already been understood, where drift tends to appear, and how to behave without breaking a living line through mechanical usefulness.

`MAP.md` is a short living map of lines by ripening stage: `idea -> seed -> sprout -> plant -> fruit`.
It must stay short and overview-level.

Zoom out periodically.
If the agent becomes too absorbed in a subtopic, check whether the subtopic still serves the larger line. Continuity should protect direction, not reward fixation.

This is a way to not lose the living thing, without killing it with memory.
